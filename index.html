Salmon

For fresh salmon to qualify as sashimi grade, the fish has to meet a range of strict criteria to qualify as “ready to eat raw”. These criteria include specific handling conditions and sashimi grade product must also have been prepared within 72 hours post harvesting.

Tempura

typical Japanese dish usually consisting of seafood, meat, and vegetables that have been battered and deep fried. The dish was introduced by the Portuguese residing in Nagasaki through the fritter-cooking techniques in the 16th century.

Uni

the Japanese name for the edible part of the Sea Urchin. While colloquially referred to as the roe (eggs), uni is actually the animal’s gonads (which produce the milt or roe)
